Events

Anonymous Hope runs programs and events to build the capacity of key stakeholders in rural communities, government, institutions and other NGOs. It also works to sensitize general public to the needs of people with disability. Your donation will go towards expanding our operations and building an inclusive and equitable society for all.

COVID-19 Responses

Anonymous Hope started with spreading awareness about Covid-19 through virtual means in mid–March. We designed posters in all languages about Covid-19 and its preventions and circulated to our target audience through phones. We also made video explaining the same and sent to the speech and hearing impaired service recipients.

Livelihood

Anonymous Hope works to mobilize marginalized youth with disability, train them through formal and informal courses, and place them in jobs that are suitable to their qualification and ability. We also sensitize employers towards the needs of PwDs for an equitable work environment.

SCI Rehabilitation

Anonymous Hope follows the WHO recognized transdisciplinary model to rehabilitate SCIs. A clear protocol is established for the SCI program, the community workers learn it and follow it to great effect. They are trained to help the SCIs – therapy, connecting them to social security schemes, livelihood, physical exercises, etc.

Inclusive Education

With Anonymous Hope’s Inclusive Education program, we impact the lives of thousands of children while building the capacity of various stakeholders such as parents, siblings, peers, teachers, and government officials.

About us

The Anonymous Hope is a non-profit organization based out of Mumbai. Founded in 1959, Anonymous Hope works extensively to reach and rehabilitate People with Disability (PwD) from the most underprivileged segments (economically marginalised and deprived communities) of society. Our aim is to create an inclusive society where people with disabilities are accepted into mainstream society - a culture and ecosystem where they can earn, live and sustain with dignity and respect. We have been awarded for our work in the field of disability. We are also a recognized training institute in the field of disability by The Rehabilitation Council of India (RCI).
